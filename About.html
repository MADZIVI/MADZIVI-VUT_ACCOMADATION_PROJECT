Accommodation Safety Tips

Your safety is our priority. Here are essential guidelines for finding and maintaining safe accommodation.

Before Visiting

  • Research the neighborhood online
  • Check crime statistics for the area
  • Visit during daylight hours first
  • Bring a trusted friend or family member
  • Verify landlord identity and credentials

During Inspection

  • Check all locks and security features
  • Test windows and door functionality
  • Verify emergency exits
  • Check for fire extinguishers and alarms
  • Ensure adequate lighting in common areas

Documentation

  • Request a written lease agreement
  • Document property condition with photos
  • Get payment receipts for all transactions
  • Keep copies of all communications
  • Record meter readings at move-in

Personal Security

  • Never carry large amounts of cash
  • Use secure payment methods
  • Vary your routines and travel times
  • Keep emergency contacts accessible
  • Trust your instincts about people and places

Safety Guidelines Comparison Table

Safety Aspect Essential Features Risk Level if Missing
Security System Locks, cameras, alarm system High
Lighting Well-lit entrances and pathways High
Location Safe neighborhood, near campus Critical
Emergency Exits Clear, accessible escape routes Critical
Landlord Verification Valid ID, references, history High
Lease Agreement Written contract, clear terms Medium

Red Flags to Avoid

- Landlord unwilling to sign a lease

- Pressure to pay cash upfront

- Vague or evasive answers about the property

- Poor maintenance and cleanliness

- No visible security measures

- Inability to provide references
